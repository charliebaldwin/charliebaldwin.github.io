MAIRU

Moonsighted is a game I developed for my senior capstone project at WPI. In the game, you play as a moth living underground, tasked with mining crystals to bring back to your village.
    I worked on the project with a group of three other students, and we completed it from start to finish in a span of seven weeks. As the Lead 3D Artist on the game, I was responsible for the majority of modeling, and virtually all of the texture work. It was an intense process but we're really happy with how it turned out!

PLAY NOW!

To reflect Moonsighted's themes of moonlight and self-destruction, our team decided to use moths as inspiration for our playable character. We designed this species, called the jonditsa in lore, to have a humanoid frame in order to make animation straightforward. Although all the jonditsa use the same mesh, this texture set was unique to the protagonist, Mairu.
    The high-poly model was sculpted in ZBrush, and retopologized using ZRemesher to save time. The wings (art by Jasmine Duerk) and antenna were added in Maya, as were the UVs. We then used Mixamo (also for time-saving purposes) to rig the model and export out the set of animations we needed for the game. I tweaked the rig inside of Maya to ensure that all of Mixamo's inherent skinning issues got cleaned up before importing into Unity.
