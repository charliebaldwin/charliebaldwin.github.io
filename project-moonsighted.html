MOONSIGHTED

Moonsighted is a game I worked on for my senior capstone project at WPI. In the game, you play as a moth living underground, tasked with mining crystals to bring back to your village.
    I worked on the project with a group of three other students, and we completed it from start to finish in a span of seven weeks. As the Lead 3D Artist on the game, I was responsible for the majority of modeling, and virtually all of the texture work. It was an intense process but we're really happy with how it turned out!

GAME DOWNLOAD

To create the environment for Moonsighted, I had to first plan out the modular kit we'd need in order to put it together. Because the game is set in a large cavern, I devised a concise set of structural rock pieces, each designed in such a way to maximize the number of ways they could be used when assembling the scene. It was also very important to consider the polycount of each asset, as we'd have hundreds of assets in the scene at a time. I made sure to use normal baking to its full extent, so that I could reduce the geometry of each model as far as I felt was possible.
   The workflow I used most for building this kit started with creating the high-poly sculpt inside of ZBrush. Making the rocks this way worked really well; I primarily used the Trim Dynamic and Orb brushes to carve out their shapes, and was able to use ZRemesher and UVMaster to get sufficient topology and UVs. I probably would have done these manually, but time restrictions required expediency and what I had worked well enough.
